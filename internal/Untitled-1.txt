# Context for Claude Code: CAD-Based Geometric Reference System

DO NOT commit this file to the repo. Internal context only.

Date: 2026-03-30

---

## What changed and why

The original study design used hand-eye calibration (AX=XB, ≥30 poses) to determine the
rigid transform between the YuMi flange and each sensor's measurement frame. This has been
replaced entirely by a CAD-based geometric reference system. The hand-eye AX=XB procedure
is removed from all documents.

The key insight: using the sensor being characterized to verify its own pose is a circular
dependency. The new system eliminates this by deriving all geometry from mechanical design.

---

## New system: how it works

### 1. Sensor fixtures (T_sensor→flange)

- Each sensor is mounted to the YuMi flange via a dedicated 3D-printed fixture
- Geometry designed in SolidWorks from the sensor's published CAD + YuMi flange spec
- T_sensor→flange is known by CAD, not derived from calibration
- Uncertainty budget contributions:
  - Fixture nominal tolerance (from SolidWorks design) — Type B, TBD per fixture
  - Fixture measured tolerance (caliper measurement of printed piece) — Type B, ± ? mm TBD by printer (unvertainty pending to be evaluated and justificated)
- CAD files and caliper logs are published outputs of the study

### 2. Sensor frame offsets (per sensor)

The CAD transform gives the housing pose. Additional corrections:

- IMU (WT901C, ICM-40609, BMI055): inertial frame coincides with housing to within
  published tolerances. No correction needed.
- Livox Mid-360: point cloud frame origin = optical center of Risley prism system.
  Offset relative to housing is published in Livox technical documentation.
  Applied as a fixed correction to T_sensor→flange.
- RealSense D455: optical frame origin and intrinsic parameters from Intel factory
  calibration, accessible via RealSense SDK. No manual intrinsic calibration required.
  Factory intrinsics used directly; nominal accuracy declared in uncertainty budget.

### 3. Target pose characterization (contact probing)

To know where the targets (AprilTag boards, reference planes) are in world coordinates:

- A conical contact tip is mounted to the YuMi flange via a CAD-known fixture
- Tip touches ≥3 non-collinear reference points on each target
- YuMi FK gives 3D coordinates of each contact point in world frame (RT = 0.10mm)
- 6DOF pose of target reconstructed analytically from ≥3 points
- Performed once before each session, with target mechanically fixed
- Contact features are geometrically unambiguous (machined edges or conical indentations,
  not printed surfaces)

For targets beyond YuMi reach (e.g. far walls for LiDAR):

- Laser rangefinder mounted in CAD-known fixture on the flange
- At each YuMi pose, beam origin and direction are known in world coordinates
- 1D distance + known TF → 3D point; ≥3 non-collinear points → full target pose
- Whether variable-distance targets are needed is pending decision

### 4. Probing repeatability characterization

- Within-session: ≥10 repeated independent touches on the same reference point
  → standard deviation of probing procedure (Type A)
- Cross-session: same procedure at start of each session → remounting uncertainty (Type A)
- Acceptance criterion: cross-session deviation < ?mm translation, < ?° rotation
  relative to previous session (uncertainy pending evaluation and justiifcaion)

### 5. Residual human operator contribution

Manual guidance of contact tip introduces a small operator contribution. This is:

- NOT eliminated
- Quantified via the probing repeatability protocol (§4 above)
- Declared in uncertainty budget as Type A contributor

### Scope boundary rule (for both documents)

**In scope:** Variables intrinsic to the sensor's physical measurement principle,
controllable or observable under 6DOF kinematic ground truth in a laboratory setting.

**Out of scope:** Variables external to the sensor, environment-dependent, or
platform-specific. Explicitly deferred to future work.

---

### IMU (WT901C, ICM-40609, BMI055)

| Variable | Status | Justification |

|----------|--------|---------------|
| Thermal drift (bias vs temperature) | IN | Intrinsic; measurable via static logs with thermal monitoring |
| Kinematic state-dependent noise (g-sensitivity, acceleration, jerk, angular rate) | IN | Captured in M4 via YuMi dynamic residuals |
| Cross-axis sensitivity | IN | Intrinsic; extracted from Six-Position Test at negligible additional cost |
| Quantization noise | IN | Deterministic from bit resolution and measurement range specs |
| Vibration from external mechanical sources | OUT — Future work | External, platform-dependent; not reproducible across setups. M4 kinematic terms provide a lower bound. |
| Magnetic interference | OUT — Future work | External, environment-dependent |
| Power supply noise | OUT — Future work | External, platform-dependent |
| Aging / long-term drift | OUT — Future work | Requires longitudinal study over months to years |

Note: The YuMi induces minimal structural vibration by design. M4 captures kinematic
state-dependent noise, not high-frequency mechanical oscillation. M4 is a lower bound
on operational noise; real deployment platforms will exceed this floor. Declared explicitly.

### Scope boundary statement (goes verbatim in RESEARCH_PLAN §2, end of section)

"This study characterizes sensor noise variables that are intrinsic to the sensor's
physical measurement principle and controllable under 6DOF kinematic ground truth in a
laboratory setting. External environmental variables — including material reflectivity,
ambient light interference, magnetic fields, power supply noise, and mechanical vibration
from operational platforms — are explicitly out of scope and identified as structured
directions for future work. The kinematic state-dependent noise model (M4) characterizes
the effect of controlled 6DOF motion on sensor output and represents a lower bound on
dynamic operational noise; real deployment platforms will exceed this floor due to
mechanical vibration sources not modeled here. This limitation is declared explicitly in
the uncertainty budget."
